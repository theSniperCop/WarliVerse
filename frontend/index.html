About Warli Art

The warli art is traditionally practiced by the warli tribe in India. It is dated back to the 10th century AD. These rudimentary wall paintings use a set of basic geometric shapes: a circle, a triangle, and a square. These shapes are symbolic of different elements of nature. The circle and the triangle come from their observation of nature. The circle represents the sun and the moon, while the triangle depicts mountains and conical trees. In contrast, the square renders to be a human invention, indicating a sacred enclosure or a piece of land.
The WarliVerse Project is based on 3D-fication of 2D Warli arts and intends to create collections in two styles - the traditional Warli style and its fusion with the modernity.
